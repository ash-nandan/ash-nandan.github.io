Neuroplasticity

Neuroplasticity is our brain's ability to adapt and recognise itself. Neuroplasticity has revolutionised our understanding of learning. It refers to our brain's ability to form new neural connections and modify existing connections based on experiences and environmental factors. Neuroplasticity is the brain's capacity to recognise its structure and functions and how it responses to learning and enviromental factors. This way, new connections can be formed and existing connections can be modified. Neuroplasticity plays an important role in different aspects of learning, memory and skill development.

Some Ways To Increase Neuroplasticity

•Focused Intention: when you pay attention to what you're doing and are highly motivated/interested in what you're focusing on; this tells your brain that there's something important there it needs to learn and adapt to.

•Movement: exercises are shown to derive neurotropic factor. You can also increase neuroplasticity through yoga, learning to play an instrument, learning a new language and making art. There's so many things you can do to increase neuroplasticity so it's good to always explore and find hobbies to help increase brain function.

•Another way you can increase neuroplasticity is experiencing something new. Maybe you can put yourself through fresh experiences and different environments.

•Another great way (believe it or not) to increase neuroplasticity is through sleep!! This was found in one of the studies; that the brain continues to learn new skills and can improve a task up to 35% just by sleeping. What Is Growth Mindset?

Having growth mindset means seeing failure as a chance to learn. It also means viewing critical feedback as a chance to improve and embrace challenging tasks. Those with growth mindset will most likely see obstacles as a chance to experiement and solve problems. Having growth mindset can help us focus on a journey of continual improvement. These are some of the examples of why having growth mindset is important.
